### D2 — Two-route governance

- **Low-trust deterministic route**: Garden's `SignalService` +
  `MaterializationRouter` may write `MemoryEntry` rows and
  `ClaimForm` rows whose only legal `claim_status` is `draft`.
- **Typed-resolution promotion route**: an attached agent calls
  `soul.resolve` with one of six resolutions; the daemon writes the
  typed audit event and transitions
  `ClaimService.transitionLifecycle(draft → active)` atomically with
  the audit row (CAS first, audit append second).
- **Out-of-band Proposal route** (unchanged from v0.3.8):
  `soul.propose_memory_update` + `soul.review_memory_proposal` for
  explicit host assertions or operator manual review.

Inspector remains an origination surface only; any apparent "promote"
action posts a typed Proposal that re-enters the governance gate.
Invariants §35 / §36 codify the two-route shape; §21 / §21b are
unchanged.

### D3 — EventLog-first atomicity

`PathRelationProposalService.propose`,
`MemoryGraphEdgeRepo.ensureEdge`, and `GraphExploreService.addEdge`
all now route through `publishEventLogMutation`. The same SQLite
transaction carries the row mutation and the EventLog append; consumers
downstream subscribe to the notification, not to the row.

## Cat-H.3 — `UpgradeAssessmentAxis` deferred closure condition

L2 did not ship the upgrade-candidate computer. The 5 fields on
`GapRecord` and `HandoffRecord` remain on the schema and continue to
be written as `null`:

- `recurrence_runs`
- `recurrence_surfaces`
- `governance_impact`
- `unresolved_age_ms`
- `upgrade_candidate`

**Closure condition.** v0.3.9 records this as a deferred decision.
The next release that closes the gap is **v0.3.10** (or, if v0.3.10
does not land an upgrade-assessment producer, the earliest v0.4.x
release that does). Until then the slot stays so the future computer
has a stable target. The closure plan for v0.3.10 must answer two
questions before flipping the decision: (1) what observable signal
populates `upgrade_candidate` (Garden re-extraction recurrence?
Inspector operator review-count?); (2) which consumer reads the
field to act on it (HealthIssueGroup severity boost? promotion-ladder
gating? recall-time priority?). If either answer is "nobody", the
fields are removed with a drop-columns migration and the slot stays
documented in maintenance-history. The plan-level fix lives in
`docs/v0.3/v0.3.9/closeout-deferred-conditions.md`.

### Recall producer does not populate plane_winning_admission (new in L0)

22. `computePlaneAttribution()` is correct + tested, but
    `RecallService` (the producer of the diagnostics sidecar) leaves
    `plane_winning_admission` as `null` on every delivered result. The
    empirical run against `public/2026-05-17T065105Z-75d418c` (1000
    delivered + 68 gold) shows all-null. Until the producer
    populates the field, the cohort-domination guard cannot fire;
    the metric ships as a measurement-ready harness with a known
    producer gap. Closure release: a recall slice that stamps
    `plane_winning_admission` per delivered result based on the
    winning admission plane (lexical / structural / embedding /
    cohort).

### Recall plane admission may be dominating top-5 slots (new in L0)

23. The post-rotation LongMemEval-S 100 archive at
    `public/2026-05-17T065105Z-75d418c` shows a striking pattern in
    its `gold[]` sub-records (aggregated by
    `scripts/analyze-recall-bias.mjs`): of 68 delivered gold
    memories, **67 (98.5%) land in `final_rank` 6-10 and ZERO land
    at rank 1**. Of those, **65 had `lexical_rank > 0.8`** — meaning
    the FTS plane found them perfectly, but some downstream scoring
    or admission step consistently demoted them past top-5.
    `plane_winning_admission` shows 38.2% of delivered gold won via
    `temporal_proximity`, suggesting that plane may be filling the
    top-5 quota with the most recently seeded haystack turns,
    crowding out lexically relevant mid-haystack gold. This is a
    much deeper finding than the dimension-sensitivity hypothesis
    (#21) — dimension rotation may have only surfaced an existing
    plane-quota / temporal-bias issue. The embedding-on rerun at
    `public/2026-05-17T104037Z-294070f` partially confirms the
    diagnosis: with the embedding plane online, 19.7% of delivered
    gold reaches `rank=1` (up from 0%) but 74.6% still lands at
    `rank 6-10`, and `temporal_proximity` still wins 33.8% of
    admissions. Embedding boosts the top of the list via semantic
    similarity but does not fix the underlying plane-admission
    skew. Worth a focused investigation in v0.3.10. Evidence sources:
    - `scripts/analyze-recall-bias.mjs` (the aggregator)
    - `docs/bench-history/public/2026-05-17T065105Z-75d418c/longmemeval-diagnostics.json` `gold[]` sub-records (embedding-off)
    - `docs/bench-history/public/2026-05-17T104037Z-294070f/longmemeval-diagnostics.json` `gold[]` sub-records (embedding-on)
    - `docs/v0.3/v0.3.9/reports/v0.3.9-bench-diff.md` §5 "Embedding plane-admission impact"

### Recall ranking sensitivity to memory dimension (new in L0)

21. The L0 bench rerun under the new producer-chain seed rotation
    (`fact / preference / decision / constraint / outcome`)
    surfaces a recall-ranking sensitivity: with a uniform `FACT`
    workspace the LongMemEval-S 100 baseline scored R@5 = 77%; with
    the diversified workspace it scores R@5 = 1.0% (R@10 still
    63.0% — hits land in the pool but not in the top-5). The drop
    is explainable in the recall scoring pipeline, not in
    persistence (all 100 returned candidates land in `warm` tier,
    `degradation_reasons` empty). The producer-side diversification
    is correct (Garden in production routes by `object_kind` into
    all four dimensions); the recall-side ranking needs tuning so
    a realistic mixed-dimension workspace does not collapse
    precision at K=5. Tracked in
    `docs/v0.3/v0.3.9/reports/v0.3.9-bench-diff.md` §5 "Recall
    sensitivity to dimension diversification". Closure release: a
    recall-scoring slice that re-weights dimension neutrality.

### Bench harness embedding-on pointer hygiene (new in L0)

24. The bench runner rewrites `docs/bench-history/public/latest-baseline.json`
    on every public-tree run, including embedding-on runs. This
    breaks the v0.3.9 Cat-D.5 design intent of keeping the embedding-off
    and embedding-on baseline chains independent (the dedicated
    `public/latest-baseline-embedding-on.json` sibling pointer was
    introduced precisely to decouple them). Observed during the L0
    embedding-on rerun (`public/2026-05-17T104037Z-294070f`): the
    runner rewrote the disabled-chain pointer and the operator had
    to revert it manually. Closure: gate the
    `latest-baseline*.json` rewrite on the embedding configuration
    detected from the archive's `kpi.json.embedding_provider` field.
    Tracked code site: `apps/bench-runner/src/longmemeval/runner.ts`
    pointer-write block.
