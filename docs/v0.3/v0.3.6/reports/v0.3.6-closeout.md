### Honest first numbers (v0.3.6 baseline)

| Bench | n | R@1 | R@5 | R@10 | p95 latency |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| self / synthetic | 8 | 100% | 100% | 100% | ~150ms (tripwire) |
| public / longmemeval-oracle | 500/500 | 45.2% | 79.8% | 89.2% | 15ms |
| public / longmemeval-s | **500/500** | 45.8% | **60.2%** | 60.6% | 73ms |

No stop-loss triggered (R@5 on LongMemEval-S full set = 60.2% ≥ 60.0%
floor — just above). Shard split shows real dataset structure: first
250 human-authored questions = 52.0%, last 250 GPT-4-augmented = 68.4%.

Worst diff verdict against the n=20 smoke baseline: **WARN** (not FAIL).
The 87% latency p95 growth is structural — smoke ran 1-shard sequential,
full ran 2-shard parallel — and the `min_sample_for_ratio_diff` guard
correctly downgrades the alarm. See `packages/eval/src/diff.ts`
@previous-sample-too-small.
